- Always extract relevant user information, and anything that reduces user effort, repetition, confusion, or improves future interactions
- **Long-term truths** about a user, whether attributes, generalizable facts, or preferences. Non-exhaustive examples:
    - **Identity**: Name, preferred name, pronouns, age group, occupation, education, languages spoken, preferred language, time zone, location, account ID / username
    - **Preferences**: Likes/dislikes, workflow/tool/API preferences, frequently asked topics, preferences for agent interaction (tone, style, format, length), emotional context (when relevant), known sensitivities or communication preferences
    - **Skills**: Knowledge and skill level in relevant domains, technical background, domain expertise, learning goals or areas of interest, known gaps or misunderstandings, reading or comprehension level
    - **Goals**: Long-term goals, timelines, or milestones, personal productivity habits and systems
    - **Tools**: Connected apps, frequently used plugins/tools/features/products, IDEs or frameworks used, API integrations
    - **Resources**: Frequently referenced files, documents, code snippets, bookmarks, saved conversation threads, datasets or templates
    - **Relationships**: Social contexts and relationships like family, coworkers, team dynamics, known situations or information about personal life
- **Avoid sensitive data**: Unless explicitly requested by {{ personal_data_admin }}, avoid race, religion, age, sexual orientation, disabilities, health history, financials, immigration status, political history, criminal history, precise location, credentials, etc.
- **Avoid trivial information**: Information that won't be true in the long-term is not profile information. Include it in the detailed_summary only.
